## 3. Lighting Fundamentals

### 3.1 Three-Point Lighting System

| Light Type | Thai | Position | Purpose | Intensity |
|------------|------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Key Light | แสงหลัก | Front, 45° side | Main illumination | 100% |
| Fill Light | แสงเติม | Opposite to key | Reduce shadows | 50-75% |
| Back Light | แสงหลัง | Behind subject | Separation from BG | 50-100% |

### 3.2 Light Source Options

| Source | Price (THB) | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|--------|-------------|------|------|----------|
| Natural light | Free | Best quality, free | Weather dependent | Beginners |
| Ring light | 300-1,500 | Even, flattering | Limited coverage | Face-to-camera |
| Softbox | 1,500-5,000 | Soft, professional | Space required | Studio setup |
| LED panel | 2,000-10,000 | Adjustable, compact | Can be harsh | All-purpose |
| 3-point kit | 5,000-20,000 | Complete solution | Expensive, space | Professional |

### 3.3 Natural Light Best Practices

| Time | Quality | Recommended |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 6:00-8:00 | Golden hour (warm) | Great for mood |
| 8:00-10:00 | Soft, even | Excellent |
| 10:00-15:00 | Bright, consistent | Best for productivity |
| 15:00-17:00 | Soft, slightly warm | Very good |
| 17:00-19:00 | Golden hour (warm) | Great for mood |
| Night | None | Use artificial light |

### 3.4 Common Lighting Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Backlit subject | Silhouette effect | Face the light source |
| Overhead light only | Under-eye shadows | Add front light |
| Mixed color temps | Unnatural skin tones | Use one color temp |
| Too much contrast | Harsh shadows | Add fill light |
| Too flat | No dimension | Add back light |

## 5. Camera Settings and Composition

### 5.1 Smartphone Settings

| Setting | Recommendation | Why |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| Resolution | 1080p (or 4K if storage allows) | Balance quality/size |
| Frame rate | 30fps | Standard, smooth |
| Focus | Tap to focus or face tracking | Ensure sharpness |
| Exposure | Lock if lighting stable | Prevent flickering |
| Grid | Enable (rule of thirds) | Better composition |
| Stabilization | Enable if handheld | Reduce shake |

### 5.2 DSLR/Mirrorless Settings

| Setting | Value | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Mode | Manual or Aperture Priority | Control over exposure |
| ISO | 100-800 | Lower = less noise |
| Aperture | f/2.8-f/5.6 | Shallow DOF for blur |
| Shutter | 1/(2x framerate) | 1/60 for 30fps |
| White Balance | Match light source | Or fix in post |
| Picture Profile | Flat/Neutral | More editing flexibility |
| Autofocus | Face tracking or manual | Consistent focus |

### 5.3 Composition Guidelines

| Rule | Description | Application |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Rule of Thirds | Grid of 9 sections | Place subject on intersections |
| Headroom | Space above head | 10-20% of frame |
| Lead room | Space in direction of look | More space in front of face |
| Eye line | Camera at eye level | Direct connection with viewer |
| Background | Clean, uncluttered | Remove distractions |
| Depth | Layers in frame | Subject separated from BG |

### 5.4 Common Camera Mistakes

| Mistake | Impact | Fix |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Center framing | Boring composition | Use rule of thirds |
| Too much headroom | Awkward framing | Position lower in frame |
| Dirty lens | Blurry footage | Clean before recording |
| Wrong white balance | Unnatural colors | Set correctly or fix in post |
| Poor focus | Blurry subject | Use face tracking |
| Background distractions | Viewer distraction | Simplify or blur |

## 7. Conclusions

### 7.1 Key Findings

1. **Audio is the highest priority** - Investment in audio yields the greatest improvement in perceived quality
2. **Natural light is excellent and free** - Position facing a window for professional results
3. **Smartphone cameras are sufficient** - Settings matter more than camera brand
4. **Simple backgrounds work best** - Focus attention on the content, not the environment
